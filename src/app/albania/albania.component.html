Tavë Kosi
Let's Make It
Origins

TavÃ« kosi - soured milk casserole - is a national dish in Albania. It is a dish of lamb and rice baked with a mixture of yogurt and eggs added to a roux. It is also popular in Greece and Turkey, where it is known as Elbasan Tava, named after the Albanian city of Elbasan.

Ingredients
  • 2lb Cubed Lamb
  • 1/3 Basmati Rice
  • 4 Eggs
  • 600ml Greek Yoghurt
  • 2 tbsp Plain Flour
  • 70g Butter
  • 4 Garlic Cloves
  • 1 tsp Oregano
  • 1 tsp Mint
  • 1 tbsp Olive Oil
  • 2 Cups Water
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 1 tsp Pepper
Method
  • Add Olive Oil and Butter to a Dutch Oven. Heat on the hob until butter has melted. Place Lamb evenly and cook until sealed.
  • Add Garlic and Rice. Cook until Garlic is softened. Add 1 cup of water and simmer until rice has absorbed most of the water.
  • Break Eggs into a large bowl and whisk. Add Yoghurt and Flour. Combine until creamy and smooth to create a Roux.
  • Add Oregano and Mint to Lamb and simmer for a few more minutes
  • Pour Roux over Lamb and cover. Place in a preheated oven for 45 minutes at 350 degrees farenheit.
