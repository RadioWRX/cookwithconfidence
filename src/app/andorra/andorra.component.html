Trinxat
Let's Make It
Origins

Trinxat is a food from the Pyrenees, principally Andorra and the Catalan comarcas of Cerdanya and Alt Urgell. It is made with potatoes, cabbage and pork meat, and resembles bubble and squeak.

The name, meaning “mashed” or “chopped”, is the past participle of the Catalan word trinxar, which means "to slice".

We Will Need

Bacon

Cabbage

Potatoes

Garlic

Salt

Black Pepper
Ingredients
  • 4 Bacon Slices
  • 1 head of Cabbage
  • 1kg of Potatoes
  • 6 Garlic Gloves
  • Salt
  • Pepper
Method
  • Clean and chop the cabbage leaves. Boil for 10 minutes.
  • Peel and dice the potatoes. Add to cabbage leaves with a pinch of salt. Boil for 20 minutes.
  • Cut the bacon into strips. Heat a little oil in a large pan. Fry the bacon and set aside.
  • In the same pan, fry the garlic.
  • Drain the potatoes and cabbage and mash together.
  • Add the cabbage and potatoes mixture to the pan with the garlic.
  • Continue to cook over a low heat for about five minutes. Season to taste. Garnish with bacon and serve.
